Harry has been dispatched from his Oslo digs to assist in the investigation of the murder of Inger Holter, a young Norwegian woman who was a minor celebrity in her native country. Holter had moved to Sydney for romantic reasons several months previously and decided to stay, never imagining that she would meet her end there by being raped and murdered. Harry is informed by Sydney's chief of detectives in precise and no uncertain terms that his role in the investigation is that of an observer and nothing more. A babysitter is assigned to Harry in the form of Andrew Kensington, the sole Aboriginal on the force. Harry takes an immediate liking to the charming man, who takes him on a tour of the highs and lows of Sydney, while the teetotaling Harry behaves himself --- for a while, anyway.<br /><br />And yes, it is a very sober and well-behaved Harry who we meet in the opening pages of THE BAT, so much so that we wonder if perhaps we have wandered off the page and into another book. There is quite a feast here, and while Nesb occasionally wanders off the heart of the matter to describe the local flora and fauna, not a bit of it is boring.
