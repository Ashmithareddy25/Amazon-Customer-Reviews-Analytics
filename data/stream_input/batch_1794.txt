If only they had mixed \\"Unmasked\\" as a rock album and not a glossy synthesizer production resembling The Human League, tempers would have abated. However with this record which is half a rock album (\\"The Oath\\" \\"Dark Light\\" Only You\\" \\"A World Without Heroes\\" Escape from the Island\\" \\"I\\"  ) and the other half a bad mix of Star Wars, Barry Manilow, a bad imitation of Iron Maiden, Abba and \\"You Light Up My Life\\" - it was considered to be the final insultby those fans who still had some hope something good would come of this. If the bad half of this album had been rejected, and the band written better, more rock oriented songs or instrumentals (think Queen doing the soundtrack of \\"Flash Gordon\\" - 1980) they would have had a winner here. A more mature rock album that would bring back the old fans and attract new listeners who worship Steely Dan. Instead the equally addled; one by drugs, and the other by greed: Bob Ezrin and Gene Simmons - couldn't wait to get the product out the door. Damn the cheesiness, the non-existent story, unlearned lessons from past failures, the often wrong but sometimes right howls of disgust from music critics and hardcore fans alike, Ace's loud and clear opposition to the stupidity of the project...GET THEM TRUCKLOADS OF RECORDS OUT TO THE WAITING MASSES PRONTO!! It was Ace who put it best: These are not bad songs. These are just not songs for Kiss. And it was cowardly and cheap of Simmons to disavow the album completely. Whatever Simmons says, Stanley implicitly supports. Peter was out of the band by then. - I've always had a soft spot for Ace as I equate him with George Harrison - who was always suppressed by the hit making duo of Lennon & McCartney - until he had a chance to record an album of his own, which proved to be at least as good, if not vastly superior than the product his former suppressors put out at that time (Harrison's \\"All Things Must Pass\\" and Frehley's 1978 solo album). Think of \\"The Elder\\" as the equivalent of \\"Their Satanic Majesties Request\\". Accept the album for what it is, and embrace the good parts, while rejecting the bad. If anything, it served as a wake up call to Simmons and Stanley to quit appeasing the drug addicts in the production room and get back to making rock records. Though port \\"Elder\\" albums never brought them the fortune they once made with their earlier albums, they still remained a strong attraction as long as they played the old favorites and they still put out one hell of a show.
