The anthemic refrain is a highlight, in which Groban sings: \\"You wanna run away run away and you can say / that it can't be so / you wanna look away look away / but you stay cause it's all so close...\\" Instrumentally, the marching snare definitely stands out.  \\"False Alarm\\" also stands out.  Here, Groban adds some falsetto.  The production continues to suit the singer's pop/classical sensibilities, featuring a thoughtful tandem of strings (arranged by David Campbell), piano/keyboards, and guitar.<br /><br />\\"Falling Slowly\\", the first cover of the effort, appears from the [[ASIN:B000PFU7OO Once]] soundtrack. Groban does the Academy-award winning number justice, though it's less alluring than the two originals that precede.  \\"She Moved Through The Fair\\" is much more captivating, even given its slow tempo and mysterious nature.  A traditional cut, producer Rob Cavallo arranges.  Groban sounds magnificent, and the cut itself grows dynamically little by little while pulling back on the third verse.  Honestly, how many cuts can you name featuring instrumentation including Uilleann Pipes? Exactly.<br /><br />\\"Below The Line\\", another original, is quicker than \\"She Moved Through The Fair\\".  \\"Below The Line\\" possessing some musical similarities to opening cut\\"Brave\\".  Horns (arranged by Rashawn Ross and Jerry Hey), add some lovely spice.  Groban delves into languages - specifically Italian - on the fine \\"E Ti Prometter&oacute;\\", one of two cuts produced by Lester Mendez. \\"E Ti Prometter&oacute;\\" translate in English to \\"I Promise You\\".  Laura Pausini guests vocally, blending well with Groban.  As with everything else, it is solid, pleasant, and enjoyable.<br /><br />Glen Campbell covered the Jimmy Webb penned \\"The Moon Is A Harsh Mistress\\" back in 1974.  Groban revisits it in 2013, yielding one of the efforts crowning achievements. Groban's vocal tone is pure and genuine.  He contrasts on the second Lester Mendez production, \\"Un Alma Mas\\", which features jazz standout Arturo Sandoval.  Sandoval's trumpet contributions are thoughtful, accentuating the Latin arrangement and complementing Groban's undeniable pipes.<br /><br />\\"Happy in Heartache\\" proceeds solidly, lacking the exceptionality of the best.  But a superb cover of Dutch band Choir of Young Believer's \\"Hollow Talk\\" (from album [[ASIN:B002G3DTYI This Is for the White in Your Eyes]]), eliminates all improprieties.  Groban makes a cut many are unaware of new, favorite addition to the playlist.  Penultimate original \\"Sincera\\" (Sincere) continues with great consistency while the Stevie Wonder cover \\"I Believe (When I Fall In Love It Will Be Forever)\\" concludes the effort on a high note.<br /><br />Overall, [[ASIN:B00A9T1HUO All That Echoes]] is another enjoyable classical-crossover effort from Josh Groban.
