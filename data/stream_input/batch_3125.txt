This is going to be a long review and I'm going to just say this right now: This is arguably the best work she has ever made, and I truly believe it transcends her above any other major pop name in America right now. The direction she has decided to take is a complete 180* from her previous album, \\"Born this Way\\", which was great in its own right, but fell short in many places. She has dropped the style that was based more on experimentation and diversity, often filled with slower ballads and strange instrumental work, and has returned to an electrically-charged electronic style that made her first album such a hit.<br /><br />This 3rd album is full of floor smashing, rock hard beats. Some of the kicks and drums layered into the under workings of each track are so aurally clear that it feels like you're listening to them in a complete sonic vacuum. The attention to detail throughout the album is on another level and it sounds like it could have only come from a production team that is completely meticulous. Lady Gaga absolutely had a ton of help on both the production and writing of the album. Among the producers are Zedd and Madeon, traditionally electro-house producers and DJ's, as well as Paul Blair, who helped her on her last two records. Many other names from piano players to jazz musicians are also on the roster. The album is both eclectic yet defining. It starts with a Kill Bill style wild west riff and after some interesting chants from Lady Gaga (LG), it builds and breaks into a beat that feels both organic and mechanical in nature. The message of the song is somewhat cheesy, but it's this playful light-hearted nature that makes Lady Gaga who she is as an artist. She's not trying to make any political statements with her music and that should always be kept in mind with music of this dance-inducing caliber. It should be noted that Infected Mushroom, a psychedelic trance group from Israel, helped produce this track. You can hear their style in some of the bizarre sounds and riffs. 9.0/10<br /><br />02 - Venus: This track is just as good the previous and LG really belts out the vocals in some parts. Those vocals feel natural and there is definitely no sort of auto-tune crap on top of it. If you've ever seen LG perform unplugged, you know that she needs no machine help to bring out her vocal essence. 9.0/10<br /><br />03 - G.U.Y.: A darker track, due to the breakbeat style production behind it. Once again, these lyrics are not breaking any political or societal boundaries, and just thinking about listening to this when wanting to do anything but move and groove is fairly humorous. Great track with a really hard beat that grooves into the soul. 8.0/10<br /><br />04 - Sexxx Dreams: Slower and less hard-hitting than almost any other track on the album. Definitely not one of my favorites, and feels like something from the previous \\"Born this Way\\" album. 6.0/10<br /><br />05 - Jewels N' Drugs (feat. Too Short, Twista, T.I.): Wow, this was something I didn't expect to see. A legendary bay area rapper who's been rapping since 1984, Too Short, and a completely fired-up wordsmith from Ohio, Twista, really rock it on this song. It's an interesting dynamic to hear LG's slow and accentuated singing right before Too Short comes in with his complete thug appeal. Unfortunately, the track is fairly abysmal for a hip hop track and the beat has that annoying top 40 sensation to it while trying to be too gangster. Nice attempt at something new, but falls short. 5.5/10<br /><br />06 - Manicure: An active track with various timings at different sections, sometimes backed by guitar, sometimes by extra drums. The lyrics on this track are mediocre and I'd actually say they do detract from the overall song, but it's still a pretty good piece with a fun beat. 7.0/10<br /><br />07 - Do What U Want (feat. R. Kelly): YES. Oh my god, yes. Here we are. The next three tracks are the best on the album. This specific track is completely phenomenal. The beat is something like a mix between an electric Eurythmics track and a 1990's slower RNB tunes. The chorus is uplifting and is filled with feeling, and the dynamic between LG and R. Kelly leaves absolutely nothing to be desired. In my opinion, this is the kind of track that proves LG is not just some Madonna clone for the modern age. It's unique, it's soulful, and it has a sound that is unmistakably her own. 10/10<br /><br />08 - Artpop: I always pray for the title track of any album to be a great representation of the album as a whole. In this case, Artpop succeeds with flying colors. It's one of the strongest tracks on the album and might objectively become the best if it withstands the test of time. Although much more laid back than the other electronic-vibed songs on the album, the beat has a mysterious and complex rhythm to it that lends itself well to a more thoughtful nature. Once again, I feel like it has sort of a \\"Sweet Dreams\\" quality to it. It's a bit of a statement towards the socialite culture, which is obviously somewhat of a pathetic thing.
