All of them are vividly realized, and Wynne Jones is an expert in taking an impartial view of these people and their personalities, letting the reader judge for themselves what they're really like (in other words, she's a master of the \\"show, don't tell\\" rule).<br /><br />But Diana Wynne Jones's real gift is in how she makes magic so domestic - and therein lies its power and charm. Whether it be Calcifer nestling in the fireplace or Howl turning his home into a flower shop, everything weird and wonderful is treated so casually by the narrative that the perspective somehow flips on itself, becoming even *more* mysterious and potent to the reader.
