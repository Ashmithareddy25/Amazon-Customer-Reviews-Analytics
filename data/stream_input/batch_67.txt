Leave a three inch tail at the beginning to tie off when the two sides meet, but otherwise, you should have enough.<br /><br />Another big issue I had was that in order to get rid of the gap on the inside, you have to constantly hold the previous threading with one finger and pull hard with the other hand. I suggest using tight gloves (gardening, golf, weight lifting, bike riding, or thin leather dress gloves etc.) if you have them.The problem is that there is a fine line between pulling it tight and pulling it too tight and the leather can and will rip a little at those holes. For those with a 2010-2011 Insight (and maybe other years), the interior dash and steering wheel plastic is usually dark blue plastic.  I'm assuming the Fit and Civics offered grey and black plastic as main options, because otherwise, I can't figure out why Honda would offer an OEM steering wheel cover in two colors (black and grey) that don't in any way match the Insight's interior.  I think of the two, the black will blend in better because it does match some minor trimming in the car such as the stereo and shift knob.  But, for the OCD, it will definitely not match perfectly. I honestly wish I had spend the extra few dollars and bought my own waxed thread in either black or grey to match the interior better.
