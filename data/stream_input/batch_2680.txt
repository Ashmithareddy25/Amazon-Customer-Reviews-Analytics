I knew it was a John Sykes project initiated just after he was given a pink slip from Whitesnake, who at the time were achieving worldwide fame.  One listen of this record though convinced me it should have been the other way around.<br /><br />With then not-as-big Bob Rock at the helm, John Sykes (vocals, guitars), Carmine Appice (drums), and Tony Franklin (bass) put out a near perfect combination of prog-rock musicianship, blues/pop-rock sensibility, and heavy metal impact.  Musically, my best description of the overall sound is that it combines a hard-rock Zeppelin style sensibility with Dio and a little Whitesnake pop sensibility thrown in.  Check out the samples and you'll get the idea.<br /><br />\\"Riot\\" starts things off rocking right away on an anthemic and almost danceable feel and straight off the bat showcases each of the players unique talents, which seques brilliantly into the slightly more funk-oriented \\"Sex Child\\".  Both songs feature some nice orchestrated keyboard work.  We then go into \\"Valley of the Kings\\" - and yes, there is a heavy Zeppelin influence in this arena prog anthem.  It took me a couple listens to get into it, but it contains some brilliant bridges and a nice harmonized outro.<br /><br />Then the one downside - \\"Jelly Roll\\" is sort of a mid-tempo boom-clap acoustic number that is a bit less to my taste.  It does add some needed breathing space after 3 intense rockers, but frankly it's the one I find myself skipping a lot of the time.<br /><br />Fear not though, as things pick right back on what is the most accessible song of this album - \\"Blue Murder\\".  Again, the band sets aside their considerable chops to deliver a hard-hitting anthemic arena rocker complete with a shout-along chorus - \\"He's screaming... Blue Murder .... / I'm scheming to Survive\\".  Like all songs to this point, it builds on layers as it goes - starting off relatively simply with just the guitar, bass, and voice and then layering additional synths and complexity.  It is this element that keeps things interesting.<br /><br />This leads into the first true ballad - \\"Out of Love\\", which showcases John Sykes' voice and allows some of the more orchestral elements of the arrangements to shine through.  The ballads in general around this period were always my least favorite element of a metal album.  Used mostly by bands trying to score additional radio play with female audiences, ballads were typically simplistic and more often than not seemed uncharacteristic of a bands sound.  \\"To Be with You\\" by  Mr. Big or \\"More than Words\\" by Extreme come to mind.  \\"Billy\\", however, successfully keeps within the theme of the album.  It sounds big, has a complex arrangement, and builds to a climax from a simple acoustic riff.  It, of course, got very little radio play.<br /><br />The last 2 selections on this record round thing out nicely, with another prog-rock anthem \\"Ptolemy\\" that further showcases the talent of the excellent musicianship on this record, followed immediately by the final track and the most energetic rocker on this record - \\"Black Hearted Woman\\".<br /><br />Some notes on production -<br />While the mix is good and you can hear everything clearly, this record had a mix similar to the Whitesnake self-titled effort (the hit making one, not the debut) - which is to say it was a little compressed and seemingly loud in places, though not distorted.  It is definitely not a clean - dry mix, but it does suit the content of the album.  One of the things I like about it is that you can here Tony Franklin's bass real well, which is fine - because his fret-less bass playing on this record is spectacular and should beshowcased.  It does at times take front and center in the mix adding a claustrophobic element to the experience.  Also, in my opinion, the cymbals on the drum kit are a little too bright.  However, these are minor quibbles.<br /><br />Unfortunately, Blue Murder would not record another album for quite some time, and the eventual follow-up \\"Nothin But Trouble\\" is unfortunately not in the same league as this release.
