It also entertainingly presents some of the most famous cases in neuroscience about people who had injuries to their brains and the result it had on their individuality. The most rip-roaring fun in this book is how the author uses memory techniques to accomplish this with the most famous being the Memory Palace. By this technique, you take a place you are very familiar with like your childhood home (the brain is better at remembering spatial and visual cues) and populate it with the most ludicrous, ridiculous and memorable images you can. For example, to memorize the order of a deck of cards, the author will start at his mailbox and put in images there to remind him that the first three cards are the two of hearts, then the king of diamonds, then the four of spades. Moonwalking with Einstein is one of these images used by the author.<br /><br />I highly recommend this book for anyone who wants to understand the science of memory and challenge yourself to memorize a whole deck of cards.
